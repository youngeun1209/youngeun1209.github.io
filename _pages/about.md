I am a postdoctoral researcher in the Department of Psychiatry and Behavioral Sciences at Stanford University. My research is in brain-computer interfaces and neural decoding: I build machine learning models that read neural signals (EEG, fMRI, and intracranial recordings) to reconstruct imagined speech and movement.

Before Stanford, I completed my Ph.D. in Brain and Cognitive Engineering at Korea University, advised by Seong-Whan Lee. My work spans real-time decoding, generative models (GANs and diffusion), and multimodal signal processing for BCI. I am a co-author of the OpenBMI toolbox, a widely used open BCI dataset, and I hold several U.S. and Korean patents on imagined-speech based interfaces.

Feel free to reach out about brain signal decoding, speech neuroprosthetics, or generative models for neural data.
